U.S. West Texas Intermediate is trading higher on Friday after OPEC and other major oil producers agreed on oil production levels starting from July. The intraday spike to the upside actually changed the main trend to up on the daily chart. According to reports, the allied partners will aim to increase production to about 1 million barrels per day (bpd). However, reports went on to say that this figure represents about two-thirds of Saudi Arabia’s initial request. Traders are saying the cartel settled on the 1 million barrel figure because some OPEC members would be unable to sufficiently ramp up crude production.
